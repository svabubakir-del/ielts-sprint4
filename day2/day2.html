Intensive farming and nutritional decline

Modern intensive farming methods have dramatically increased the volume of food available to consumers worldwide. However, scientists have found that crops grown using these methods often contain fewer nutrients than those produced a generation ago. One notable reason for this is that plants grown too rapidly are unable to absorb essential minerals, including vital trace elements, before they are harvested. The resulting food may appear healthy but has been significantly diluted in terms of its true nutritional value. To minimise the damage caused by soil degradation, some farmers are returning to traditional crop rotation techniques. These methods help replenish depleted soils naturally, without the need for synthetic preservatives or artificial inputs. Despite being maligned by large agricultural corporations for many years, organic farming is now recognised as a sustainable alternative capable of restoring yields without further depleting essential minerals from the earth.

Decide True or False. Then write the word in the text that means the same (statements 1–3) or the opposite (statements 4–5) of the bold word.
